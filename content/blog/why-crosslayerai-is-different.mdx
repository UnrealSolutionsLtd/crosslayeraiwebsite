That amazing NPC who remembered your character's backstory? Gone until you launch the game again. The AI has no way to reach out, no presence outside the game world, no ability to re-engage players who've drifted away.

## Honest Limitations: What to Consider

No solution is perfect, and we believe in transparency. Here are the trade-offs to consider:

### Integration Complexity
- **Initial setup requires thought** - Unlike plug-and-play analytics, CrossLayerAI needs meaningful event data to work its magic. You'll need to instrument key moments (boss fights, clutch plays, emotional beats) for the companion to reference them later.
- **Cross-platform identity** - Connecting a player's in-game identity to their Discord account requires user opt-in and OAuth flows. It's not difficult, but it's an extra step.

### Learning Curve for Teams
- **New mental model** - Thinking about player moments as "stories to tell later" is different from traditional analytics. Your team needs to shift from "what metrics matter" to "what moments matter."
- **Content strategy required** - The AI companion needs voice guidelines, personality constraints, and moderation rules. Studios used to silent analytics dashboards will need to think about brand voice.

### Early-Stage Platform
- **We're building alongside early partners** - CrossLayerAI is actively evolving. Early adopters get direct input on features, but also encounter a platform that's still maturing.
- **Channel coverage expanding** - Discord integration is solid; other platforms (TikTok, Reddit, etc.) are in development. Your roadmap needs may not align perfectly with ours yet.

For studios willing to invest in thoughtful integration, these trade-offs unlock capabilities no other platform offers. For those seeking zero-effort solutions, traditional tools may be a better short-term fit.
